LLMs ‚Äî Core Concept
What Is an LLM?

The Technical Reality

A Large Language Model is a deep neural network ‚Äî typically a Transformer architecture ‚Äî trained on massive text corpora via self-supervised learning. During training, the model learns to predict the next token (word fragment) given all prior context.

The "large" refers to parameter count: frontier models contain hundreds of billions of learned numerical weights encoding statistical relationships across virtually all human-written knowledge. What emerges is not just pattern matching ‚Äî but reasoning, summarization, and domain expertise.

Why This Matters in Finance
The same architecture that completes sentences can also complete reasoning chains ‚Äî weighing evidence, identifying inconsistencies in documents, and flagging anomalies in text-based data.
Financial text ‚Äî contracts, filings, customer notes ‚Äî is exactly the kind of dense, structured prose LLMs were built to process.

LLMs ‚Äî Critical Gaps
Limitations in Finance

The Three Gaps That Drive the Stack

Knowledge cutoff: An LLM trained on last year's data doesn't know today's interest rates, recent regulatory changes, or current market conditions. In finance, stale information is dangerous.

No private data access: The model has never seen your loan book, your customer profiles, your internal credit policies, or your transaction history. It can only use what's in its prompt.

Hallucination: Confident fabrication of facts is catastrophic in regulated environments. An LLM that invents a regulatory requirement or a client's account balance is a compliance liability.

The Solutions

  • Knowledge cutoff‚Üí RAG with live data
  • Private data‚Üí RAG over your corpus
  • Hallucination‚Üí Grounding + citations
  • Taking actions‚Üí Agentic AI
  • System integration‚Üí n8n orchestration

What Is n8n?

The Orchestration Layer

n8n is a visual workflow automation tool where each node represents a step: a trigger, a data transformation, an API call, a database query, or an AI operation. Nodes connect to form pipelines that run automatically.

What separates n8n from simpler tools: complex branching logic, error handling, loops, webhook endpoints, custom JS/Python code nodes, and native AI integrations ‚Äî making it viable for production-grade financial operations, not just simple automations.

Self-Hosted AI for Regulated Entities

Why Self-Hosting Matters in Finance

Sending customer PII, account data, or transaction records to a third-party SaaS workflow tool creates immediate data residency and GDPR risk exposure. n8n's self-hosted deployment eliminates this ‚Äî your data never leaves your infrastructure perimeter.

For FCA, SEC, or MAS regulated firms, this distinction is often the difference between a permissible deployment and a regulatory breach. Open-source auditability also satisfies internal model governance requirements that cloud-black-box tools cannot.

The shift from AI that answers to AI that acts. In fintech, agentic systems don't just flag a suspicious transaction ‚Äî they investigate it, cross-reference it, draft a report, and route it ‚Äî autonomously.

The difference between an LLM and an agent: an LLM responds once and stops. An agent loops ‚Äî it uses its output to decide what to do next, calls tools, observes results, and iterates until the goal is met. In financial services, this is the difference between a smart assistant and an autonomous operator.
